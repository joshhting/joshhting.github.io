RRT

Path-planing algorithm for Georgia Tech RoboJackets

RRT stands for Rapidly-exploring Random Tree, a path planning algorithm commonly used in autonomous robotic systems. The algorithm fills the search space with a randomly branching tree starting at the start state, expanding until the goal state is reached. This implementation is used by Georgia Tech's RoboJackets team in RoboCup, an international research competition in which teams develop fleets of robots that autonomously play soccer. Source

Mobius Score

Piano sheet music generator

Heavily based on Anthem Score, Mobius Score was created to auto-generated piano sheet music from mp3 files of piano music. This is accomplished via a TensorFlow network; mp3 files are parsed for note features, which are then passed into Tensor Flow to determine the most likely note or chord. This is still a work in progress and requires a lot of traning data to work effectively. Source

Mobius Tunes

A music playlist that mixes it up

Mobius Tunes will play a playlist of music just like any other music player, but it identifies points in your songs that sound similar. Rather than finish playing each song entirely and sequentially, it will randomly jump between these similar locations in different songs, allowing for a unique listening experience each time. Source
